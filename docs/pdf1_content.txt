1. Introduction  
1.1 Purpose  
This document defines the functional and non -functional requirements for the Helpdesk Ticket 
Management System (HTMS) to be deployed by the Ministry of Foreign and Diasp ora Affairs of 
the Republic of Kenya. The system will support all 70 Kenyan diplomatic missions worldwide, 
enabling staff to raise, track, and resolve issues across IT, HR, and facilities departments from a 
centralised platform.  
 
1.2 Scope  
The HTMS will be a web -based application accessible from any modern browser. It will serve 
three categories of users — mission staff, departmental agents/technicians, and HQ 
administrators — and will provide structured, category -routed ticket management wi th real -time 
notifications and executive reporting dashboards.  
 
1.3 Definitions & Acronyms

3. Functional Requirements  
3.1 User Authentication & Access Control  
• All users shall authenticate via a secure login (username and password) with support for 
password reset via email.  
• Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) shall enforce that each user sees only w hat their 
role permits.  
• Mission isolation: staff at a given mission can only view and interact with tickets 
belonging to their mission.  
• HQ Super Admins can view and manage all missions, tickets, and users system -wide.  
• Sessions shall expire after a configur able period of inactivity (default: 30 minutes).  
• The system shall support multi -factor authentication (MFA) as an optional enhancement.  
 
3.2 Ticket Submission  
• Any authenticated Requester shall be able to submit a new ticket via a web form.  
• Ticket submission form shall capture: Title, Description, Category (IT / HR / Facilities / 
Other), Priority (Low / Medium / High / Critical), Attachments (images, documents — 
max 10MB per file).  
• Upon submission, the system shall auto -assign a unique ticket  ID and timestamp.  
• The ticket shall be automatically routed to the appropriate departmental queue based on 
the selected category.  
• Requesters shall receive an immediate email and in -app notification confirming ticket 
submission with the ticket ID.

Every ticket shall progress through the following defined states:

3.4 Ticket Routing & Assignment  
• Upon submission, tickets shall be automatically routed to the relevant departmental 
queue: IT tickets to IT team, HR tickets to HR team, Facilities tickets to Facilities team.  
• Agents may be assigned tickets manually by Mission Admin or HQ Admin, or via auto -
assignment (round -robin within department).  
• Agents can reassign tickets to other agents within the same department.  
• Escalat ion: tickets not assigned within a configurable period shall be escalated and 
flagged for Mission Admin review.  
 
3.5 Ticket Collaboration & Comments  
• Agents and Requesters shall be able to add comments/updates to a ticket thread.  
• All comments shall be times tamped and attributed to the user who added them.  
• Internal notes visible only to Agents and Admins (not the Requester) shall be supported.  
• File attachments may be added to comments.

throughout its lifecycle. This ensures full accountability and transparency across all 70 missions.

3.7 Not ifications  
• The system shall send both in -app and email notifications for the following events:  
• New ticket submitted (to Agent/Admin of relevant department).  
• Ticket assigned to an agent (to that agent).  
• Ticket status changed (to Requester).  
• New comment added to a ticket (to all parties involved).  
• Ticket resolved (to Requester, prompting confirmation).  
• SLA breach warning (to Mission Admin and HQ Admin).  
• Notification preferences shall be configurable per user (opt -in/opt -out per event type).  
 
3.8 Search & Filtering  
• All users shall be able to search tickets by ticket ID, keyword, category, status, or date 
range — within their permitted scope.  
• Agents and Admins shall be able to filter ticket queues by status, priority, category, and 
assigned agent . 
• Search results shall be paginated and sortable.

3.9 HQ Dashboard & Reporting  
• HQ Super Admins shall have access to a global analytics dashboard displaying:

4. Advanced Functional Requirements  
The following requirements address the complex operational realities of running a  diplomatic 
helpdesk across 70 globally distributed missions. These go beyond basic ticketing and are 
critical for the system to function correctly in a real -world Ministry environment.  
 
4.1 Complex SLA Calendars & Localised Working Hours  
Standard countdow n timers cannot be used for SLA management across missions operating in 
different countries with different working weeks and public holidays.  
 
• The Problem: The working week in Riyadh and Doha missions is Sunday to Thursday, 
while London and Washington oper ate Monday to Friday. Missions also observe both 
Kenyan public holidays (e.g., Jamhuri Day, Madaraka Day) and the official public 
holidays of their host country.  
• Each mission shall be configured with its own localised working hours schedule 
specifying: wor king days of the week, daily start and end times, and the mission local 
timezone.  
• The SLA countdown timer shall automatically pause outside of the mission configured 
working hours including nights, weekends as defined per mission, and public holidays.  
• The system shall maintain a dynamic holiday calendar per country, configurable by HQ 
Super Admins and updateable annually.  
• SLA breach warnings and escalations shall only trigger during active working hours for 
the relevant mission.  
• The SLA configuration dashbo ard shall display per mission: effective working hours, 
active timezone, and next upcoming public holiday.

4.2 Asset & Inventory Linking  
Tickets that lack device context create unnecessary back -and-forth between requesters and 
agents, slowing down resolution times.  
 
• The system shall include an Asset Management module maintaining a register of all 
government -owned devices and equipment per m ission.  
• Each asset record shall capture: Government Inventory Tag (e.g., MFA -LPT-204), 
Device Type, Make and Model, Operating System and Version, Location within the 
mission, Assigned User, Purchase Date, and Warranty Expiry.  
• When submitting a ticket, a Re quester shall be able to search for and link the specific 
asset using the inventory tag or device name.  
• Upon linking an asset to a ticket, the responding agent shall immediately see the full 
asset profile without needing to ask follow -up questions.  
• Mission  Admins and HQ Super Admins shall be able to add, edit, and decommission 
assets in the register.  
• The system shall flag assets that are out of warranty or have a history of more than 3 
tickets in 90 days as candidates for replacement.  
• Asset history shall sh ow all tickets ever raised against a specific device.  
 
4.3 Out -of-Office & Delegation Workflows  
Senior diplomatic officials including High Commissioners, Ambassadors, and the Principal 
Secretary are frequently travelling and unavailable for approvals, which can stall critical tickets.  
 
• The system shall support a delegation feature that allows any use r with approval 
authority to temporarily transfer that authority to a designated deputy.  
• When setting up a delegation, the user shall specify: the deputy, the start and end date 
of the delegation, and the scope such as all approvals or limited to specific categories 
like procurement or access requests.

4.4 Secure File Handling & Attachment Sanitisation  
Government networks a re high -value targets. File uploads are a common attack vector for 
delivering malicious payloads disguised as legitimate documents or images.  
 
• All file attachments shall be strictly limited by MIME type. Only the following formats shall 
be accepted: PDF, J PEG, PNG, and Microsoft Word (.docx). No executable, script, or 
archive files shall be permitted under any circumstance.  
• Individual file size shall be capped at 10MB per attachment, with a maximum of 5 
attachments per ticket.  
• The system shall validate MIME  type on the server side, not solely based on file 
extension, to prevent spoofed file types.  
• In the production environment, all uploaded files shall be routed through an automated 
virus -scanning pipeline (e.g., ClamAV or equivalent) before being written to  storage.  
• Files that fail the virus scan shall be rejected, the upload blocked, the incident logged in 
the audit trail, and the Mission Admin and HQ Security team notified immediately.  
• Stored attachments shall not be publicly accessible via direct URL. All  downloads shall 
be served through authenticated, time -limited signed links.  
 
4.5 Localisation & Internationalisation (i18n)  
Kenyan missions employ Locally Engaged Staff (LES) including drivers, maintenance crews, 
local IT contractors, and administrative s taff who may not be fluent in English. Requiring 
English -only access excludes these staff from using the system effectively.  
 
• The user -facing ticket submission portal shall support Internationalisation (i18n), 
enabling the interface to toggle to a preferre d language.  
• Version 1.0 shall support the following languages in addition to English: French 
(Francophone Africa and Paris), Arabic (Middle East and North Africa), and Mandarin 
Chinese (Beijing mission).  
• Language selection shall be available on the login screen and in the user profile settings.  
• Core interface elements to be translated include: navigation menus, form labels, status 
labels, notification messages, and error messages.

4.6 On -Behalf -Of (OBO) Ticketing  
High-ranking officials such as Ambassadors,  High Commissioners, and the Principal Secretary 
do not typically interact directly with operational systems. Their administrative assistants and 
protocol officers act as their primary interface.  
 
• The system shall provide an On -Behalf -Of (OBO) ticketing fe ature, allowing authorised 
administrative assistants or protocol officers to submit and manage tickets in the name 
of a senior official.  
• OBO relationships shall be configured by Mission Admins or HQ Super Admins. A staff 
member must be explicitly authorise d to act on behalf of a specific official.  
• When submitting an OBO ticket, the assistant shall select the official from a pre -
approved list. The ticket shall display both names, for example: Submitted by Ms. 
Achieng on behalf of H.E. Ambassador Kamau.  
• OBO t ickets shall automatically inherit the priority level associated with the senior official 
role. Tickets submitted on behalf of an Ambassador or Principal Secretary shall be 
flagged as High or Critical priority by default.  
• The official on whose behalf a tic ket was raised shall receive all notifications related to 
that ticket, in addition to the submitting assistant.  
• All OBO actions shall be fully recorded in the audit trail, clearly distinguishing between 
the submitting assistant and the named official.  
• The senior official shall retain the ability to log in independently and view all tickets 
raised on their behalf.  
 
5. Non -Functional Requirements  
5.1 Performance  
• The system shall support at least 500 concurrent users across all missions.  
• Page load time shall n ot exceed 3 seconds on a standard broadband connection.  
• Ticket submission and status updates shall be reflected in the system within 5 seconds.  
 
5.2 Availability & Reliability

maximum).  
• Scheduled maintenance windows shall be communicated at least 48 hours in advance.  
• Automated database backups shall occur daily, with the ability to restore within 4 hours.  
 
5.3 Security  
• All data in transit shall be encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher (HTTPS).  
• All data at rest shall be encrypted using AES -256 or equivalent.  
• The system shall comply with data protection best practices (minimal data collection, 
access logging, user consent).  
• All user actions shall be logged for audit purposes with timestamps and IP addresses.  
• The system shall be protected against common web vulnerabilities (OWASP Top 10) 
including SQL injection, XSS, and CSRF.  
 
5.4 Usability  
• The interface shall be accessible via  any modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, 
Safari) without requiring plugin installation.  
• The system shall be mobile -responsive for access on tablets and smartphones.  
• The agent and admin interfaces shall use English as the primary language. The user -
facing submission portal shall support French, Arabic, and Mandarin in addition to 
English, as defined in Section 4.5.  
• All key user workflows (submit ticket, update status, view dashboard) shall be 
completable within 3 clicks/steps.  
 
5.5 Scalability  
• The sys tem architecture shall support scaling to accommodate additional missions 
beyond the initial 70 without major re -architecture.  
• The database design shall handle a growing ticket archive without performance 
degradation.

8. Constraints & Assumptions  
8.1 Constraints  
• Agent and Admin interfaces operate in English only. The submission portal supports 
English, French, Arabic, and Mandarin as defined in Section 4.5.  
• Mission staff can only view their own mission's tickets — cross -mission visibility is 
restricted to HQ Super  Admins.  
• The system will not integrate with external HR or payroll systems in version 1.0.  
• Internet connectivity at missions is assumed; the system will not support offline -first 
operation in v1.0.

8.2 Assumptions  
• Each mission will have at least one Mission Admin user configured at system launch.  
• HQ IT team will manage user provisioning and mission setup during onboarding.  
• An SMTP email service will be available for sending notification emails.  
• The Ministry will pro vide or procure suitable server/cloud hosting infrastructure.  
 
9. Out of Scope (Version 1.0)  
• Mobile native applications (iOS / Android) — web responsive interface only in v1.0.  
• Integration with third -party HR, payroll, or ERP systems.  
• Offline / low -connectivity mode.  
• AI-powered ticket categorisation or smart routing.  
• Customer satisfaction (CSAT) surveys.  
The above features may be considered for future versions based on user feedback and Ministry 
priorities.  
 
10. Acceptance Criteria  
The system will be considered ready for deployment when:  
• All five ticket statuses (Open, Assigned, In Progress, Resolved, Closed) function 
correctly end -to-end. 
• Department -based routing correctly assigns tickets to the appropriate queue for IT, HR, 
and Facilit ies categories.  
• Mission isolation is verified — a user from Mission A cannot view tickets from Mission B.  
• Both email and in -app notifications are sent reliably for all defined trigger events.  
• HQ dashboard displays accurate aggregated data from all missions . 
• Reports can be exported as PDF and CSV.  
• The system passes security testing against the OWASP Top 10 vulnerabilities.  
• User Acceptance Testing (UAT) is completed with representatives from at least 3 
missions and HQ.
