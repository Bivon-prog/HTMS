Ministry of Foreign and Diaspora Affairs of Kenya
 Global Ticket System — Design Discussion Notes
 
1. Project Overview
The Ministry of Foreign and Diaspora Affairs of Kenya is planning a staff-facing internal helpdesk ticket
system to serve its 70 missions worldwide — including embassies, high commissions, and consulates. The
first version will be for internal staff use only, with a future version planned to extend access to Kenyan
citizens abroad.
2. Two-Tier Resolution Model
Tickets will be handled at two levels depending on the nature of the issue:

3. Time Zone Management
With 70 missions spanning multiple continents, time zone handling is one of the key technical challenges.
The following approach was identified:
Core principle:
 All timestamps stored in UTC in the database.
 Each mission and user has a configured local time zone.
 The system displays times in the user's local time zone.
 SLAs for escalated (Tier 2) tickets are calculated based on HQ Nairobi business hours.
 Tier 1 ticket SLAs run on the mission's local working hours.
Example: A ticket submitted from Tokyo is escalated to HQ. The system communicates the expected
resolution time in Tokyo local time, calculated from Nairobi business hours.
4. Escalation Logic
Two escalation triggers have been defined:
4.1 Automatic Escalation
 A countdown timer tracks unresolved tickets based on the mission's local business hours.
 If the timer expires, the ticket is automatically moved to the HQ department queue.
 Both HQ and the originating mission are notified.
 The SLA clock resets or continues based on Nairobi HQ business hours.
 Different ticket categories have different escalation thresholds (e.g., email/account issues escalate
faster than general HR matters).

4.2 Manual Escalation
 The mission focal person can manually escalate a ticket to HQ at any time.
 A reason or note must be provided before escalation.
 HQ is notified immediately regardless of time.
 The full ticket history, attachments, and notes travel with the escalated ticket.

5. Role-Based Access & Visibility
The system uses role-based visibility to ensure each user sees only relevant tickets:
Role
Ticket Visibility
Mission Staff
Only their own submitted tickets
Mission Focal Person
All tickets from their mission
HQ Department (IT, HR, Finance, etc.)
Only tickets routed to their department
System Administrator
Everything across all 70 missions
6. Smart Ticket Routing at Escalation
When a ticket is escalated to HQ, the system automatically routes it to the correct department based on its
category. For example, an 'Email Recovery' ticket is routed directly to the IT department queue — Finance
or HR never see it.
Open design questions to resolve:
 Miscategorized tickets — how does an HQ department reroute a ticket to the correct department?
 Cross-department tickets — can a ticket belong to or be shared between multiple departments?
 Mission Focal Person — is there one per mission, or can there be multiple focal persons by
department?
7. Future Roadmap
Version 2 of the system is planned to include public-facing access for Kenyan citizens abroad, enabling
them to submit consular and diaspora service requests directly through the platform.
